## 3Jane

### Project Overview and Purpose

3Jane is a groundbreaking DeFi protocol that represents a fundamental shift in how the industry approaches lending. Rather than relying on the traditional over-collateralization model that has dominated decentralized finance, 3Jane introduces credit-based unsecured lending powered by comprehensive creditworthiness assessments that combine on-chain activity, off-chain financial data, and traditional credit scores.

3Jane positions itself as a "credit-based peer-to-pool money market" designed to revolutionize DeFi lending by providing algorithm-driven, real-time unsecured USDC credit lines. The protocol's core mission addresses a critical inefficiency in the DeFi market: despite the space reaching over $150 billion in size, the overcollateralization requirement remains a significant barrier to mainstream adoption.

The project targets a sophisticated user base including liquidity miners, traders, arbitrageurs, enterprises, and AI agents—users who traditionally face inefficiencies in traditional overcollateralized lending where they must deposit 100% in collateral to borrow 50% in value. By decoupling lending from asset holdings and grounding it instead in creditworthiness, 3Jane aims to unlock the trillion-dollar institutional lending market that remains largely untapped by current DeFi protocols.

### Recent Developments and News

**Junior Tranche Coverage**

3Jane's loan structure features 100% coverage through junior tranche Total Value Locked (TVL), providing a robust safety mechanism for senior lenders. This structure creates risk tranching where junior capital absorbs initial losses, protecting senior tranches and creating attractive risk-adjusted returns for different investor profiles.

**USD3 and Pendle Integration**

The protocol has integrated USD3 for boosted Pendle yields, enabling lenders to earn enhanced returns through Pendle's yield optimization mechanisms. This integration allows 3Jane users to access sophisticated yield strategies while maintaining the protocol's core unsecured lending functionality.

**Mainnet Launch**

According to available information, 3Jane announced plans to launch its mainnet in early November. The protocol was originally planned for Base Network but was ultimately designed for Ethereum, potentially due to considerations of liquidity and ecosystem maturity.

### Technology and Innovation

The technological foundation of 3Jane represents a sophisticated fusion of on-chain and off-chain systems. The protocol's innovation centers on several key components:

**Jane Score and Credit Assessment**

3Jane has developed a comprehensive credit scoring system called Jane Score, which combines both on-chain and off-chain creditworthiness assessments. The on-chain component integrates data from two established scoring protocols: Cred Score and Blockchain Bureau Score, which analyze user behavior patterns including borrowing history, liquidation events, holdings, and exchange interactions across EVM chains. The off-chain component incorporates traditional credit data from VantageScore 3.0, Equifax, and TransUnion—two of the three major US credit bureaus.

**3Jane Credit Risk Algorithm (3CA)**

At the heart of the protocol lies the 3CA, which captures user interaction data across DeFi platforms, centralized exchanges (CEXs), and traditional banking systems. The algorithm underwrites credit limits based on a user's Jane Score and asset type, with default penalty mechanisms that restrict access and increase interest rates for bad actors. This system is sophisticated enough to detect artificial asset inflation attempts, where users might externally borrow to artificially enhance their credit profile.

**Privacy and Compliance Stack**

3Jane employs zkTLS (zero-knowledge Transport Layer Security) protocol to create a "privacy compliance stack" that enables trustless verification of off-chain data without exposing sensitive information. This technology allows the protocol to integrate traditional financial data while maintaining privacy and meeting regulatory requirements.

**Data Integration and Recovery Mechanisms**

The protocol binds users' bank account data to their Ethereum addresses through Plaid, with end-to-end encryption and optional deletion after repayment. In case of default, 3Jane implements a sophisticated recovery mechanism: the protocol auctions non-performing loans on-chain, allowing U.S. debt collection agencies to intervene, with Ethereum smart contracts ensuring immutable default records.

## Goblintown

### Project Overview and Purpose

Goblintown is a satirical NFT collection launched in May 2022 on the Ethereum blockchain, featuring 10,000 unique goblin-themed NFTs. The project was explicitly designed as an anti-NFT initiative, created to mock and troll traditional blue-chip NFT projects like Bored Apes, Mutant Apes, Moonbirds, Clone X, and Invisible Friends.

From its inception, the creators were transparent about their approach, stating boldly on the official website: "No roadmap. No Discord. No utility. CC0." This CC0 (Creative Commons Zero) licensing is particularly significant, as it grants NFT holders complete creative freedom to use their goblin NFTs in any manner they choose, removing traditional copyright restrictions.

The project fundamentally challenged the prevailing NFT ethos that emphasized utility and long-term development roadmaps. Instead, Goblintown embraced irreverence and absurdity as its core value proposition, arriving at a time when the NFT community was experiencing significant financial losses and emotional fatigue from the 2022 market crash.

Goblintown launched anonymously, but the creators later identified themselves as Truth Labs, a collective of blockchain creators and artists. Truth Labs describes its mission as "dedicated to sharing delightful blockchain mischief, exploring creatively, developing rich, fun worlds and experiences (both IRL and in the digital realm), and providing a platform for new voices and visions in this space." Prior to Goblintown, Truth Labs created The 187 and Illuminate Collective, establishing a pattern of unconventional NFT projects.

### Recent Developments and News

Recent developments for Goblintown include a Tikit trade-in event for Truth NFTs at original price (with NFTs being burned in the process) and an extension of Shweepshtakes with new characters. These initiatives demonstrate Truth Labs' continued engagement with the community and expansion of the Goblintown ecosystem.

In September 2022, Truth Labs announced the launch of Truth Labs Marketplace, a dedicated secondary sales platform for its NFT collections, including Goblintown. This represented a strategic move to reduce transaction costs for users, with the Truth Labs Marketplace charging a single 5% royalty-marketplace fee compared to competing platforms like OpenSea which charge a 2.5% marketplace fee plus creator royalties ranging from 2.5-10%.

### Technology and Innovation

Goblintown operates on the Ethereum blockchain with ERC-721 token standards. The project's most significant innovation was not technological but philosophical: the CC0 licensing model that grants holders complete creative freedom without copyright restrictions.

The Truth Labs Marketplace represents a technical infrastructure development aimed at creating a cost-efficient trading environment for the Truth Labs ecosystem. This initiative reflected broader Web3 industry reconsideration of marketplace fees and royalty structures, positioning Truth Labs as a cost-conscious alternative to established platforms like OpenSea, Rarible, and LooksRare.

### Market Performance and Metrics

Goblintown experienced extraordinary success despite—or perhaps because of—its anti-commercial stance:

**Launch and Initial Growth**

The collection launched during the week of May 20, 2022, and sold out in just days with all 10,000 freely minted pieces claimed, with only transaction fees required. The project's rapid ascent was remarkable given zero marketing investment, no partnerships with established brands, and no prominent NFT community endorsements.

**Price Evolution**

Following its rapid sellout, Goblintown's floor price climbed dramatically:
- Initial floor: Over 2 ETH ($2,500)
- Mid-range floor: 4 ETH ($4,800)
- Peak floor: 9 ETH according to some sources

Individual NFTs commanded substantial premiums, with sales reaching tens of thousands of dollars. One notable transaction was Goblintown #8995, which sold for $136,440.

### Community and Adoption

The community aspect of Goblintown became one of its defining characteristics. The project's irreverent "nothing really matters" tone resonated deeply with collectors fatigued by financial losses and the intensity of the broader NFT space. This emotional connection transformed curiosity and FOMO into a thriving, organic community.

**In-Person Community Building**

The strength of the community was demonstrated at NFT.NYC 2022, where the Goblintown community met en-masse for the first time, with numerous attendees wearing goblin garb and proudly representing their community. This represented a significant transition from digital-only engagement to real-world community formation.

The community has maintained engagement through various initiatives including the Shweepshtakes program and Tikit trade-in events, demonstrating sustained interest beyond the initial hype cycle.

The development of the Truth Labs Marketplace also represents a strategic partnership model where Truth Labs maintains control over its ecosystem's trading infrastructure rather than relying solely on third-party platforms.

### Risks and Challenges

Several inherent risks characterize the Goblintown project:

**Sustainability Questions**: The project's lack of a roadmap and explicit rejection of utility raise questions about long-term viability and whether the initial viral momentum can be sustained.

The project's willingness to remain "anti-establishment" while evolving may prove to be its greatest strategic advantage.

The Pepe NFT ecosystem encompasses two major initiatives: Rare Pepe and Fake Rares, representing different eras of digital art and blockchain innovation centered around the iconic Pepe the Frog meme.

Rare Pepe originated in September 2016 when an anonymous user called "Mike" created three Pepe trading cards backed by the Counterparty protocol, an application layer built on the Bitcoin blockchain. This project fundamentally pioneered the concept of tokenized digital art and NFTs, establishing many standards and values that would become foundational to the broader NFT ecosystem. The project operated as a community-driven platform where hundreds of artists from around the world could submit and trade their digital Pepe artwork. The Rare Pepe directory closed in early 2018, marking the end of the original project's active minting period.

Fake Rares emerged in 2021 as a natural successor and spiritual continuation of the Rare Pepe movement. Initiated by Rare Scrilla, an artist and collector active in the original Rare Pepes community, Fake Rares was created in response to a wave of Pepe NFT minting on Ethereum during the 2021 bull market. Rather than competing with these Ethereum-based initiatives, Fake Rares deliberately brought the Pepe community back to Bitcoin's Counterparty protocol, honoring the roots of the original project. The project serves as both a parody of and genuine homage to the Rare Pepe legacy, embracing the absurdity and whimsical nature of internet culture while establishing itself as a collectible movement in its own right.

### Recent Developments and News

The FAKE RARES collection features releases including FAKEBIRTH and FAKEVOID, representing the project's continued creative output in December 2025. Fake Rares has entered its fourth year of operation, continuing to be a space for artists creating Pepe memes on Bitcoin.

In 2021, founding members of Rare Pepe infused the project with new creative energy, producing more than 250 digital works, many of which are animated and include original soundtracks. Artnet Auctions has hosted on-chain sales celebrating the Rare and Fake Pepe movements, recognizing the innovative spirit of both projects.

Additionally, Vincent Van Dough announced a project called Notable Pepes, which brings a new twist to the classic Rare Pepes and Fake Rares model through a lottery-ticket mechanism where holders can mint weekly Pepe art drops, bridging communities across both Counterparty and Ethereum platforms.

### Technology and Innovation

The technological foundation distinguishing Pepe NFTs lies in their blockchain infrastructure choices:

**Counterparty Protocol**: Both Rare Pepe and Fake Rares utilize Counterparty, an application layer built on the Bitcoin blockchain rather than dedicated blockchain platforms. This choice represents a philosophical commitment to Bitcoin's established security and decentralization. Counterparty enabled the creation of native digital assets directly on Bitcoin, predating the current wave of Ethereum-based NFTs by years.

**Wallet Innovation**: The technical development of infrastructure was groundbreaking for its time. Joe Looney, the original creator of the Rare Pepe Wallet, later developed a corresponding Fake Rare Wallet to accommodate the new project's growth. These wallets consolidated Pepe cards and their associated images in centralized interfaces, which was considered innovative infrastructure at the time.

**Token Utilities**: Rare Pepes utilized Pepe Cash as a native utility token for transactions within the ecosystem. Similarly, Fake Rares employ FAKEASF tokens with utility comparable to emerging Bitcoin-based token systems like Runes.

**Decentralized Curation**: The Fake Rares directory is guarded by La Faka Nostra, an undisclosed group of individuals who determine the composition of each series release, maintaining quality and community standards.

### Market Performance and Metrics

The Pepe NFT ecosystem has demonstrated significant market value:

**Historic Auction Records**: A Rare Pepe card titled HOMERPEPE (2016) set a precedent by selling for 350,000 PepeCash, equivalent to $39,200 at the time of the Rare Art Fest auction in January 2018. This sale was instrumental in putting NFT concept on the global map.

The same HOMERPEPE card subsequently changed hands for $320,000, and ultimately was included in Sotheby's "Natively Digital 1.2" NFT auction in October 2021. Another Rare Pepe, PEPENOPOULOS (2016), closed as the highest lot at $3.65 million in that Sotheby's sale, marking significant value appreciation.

**Contemporary Pricing**: Since the NFT explosion of 2021, early Rare Pepe cards have traded for upwards of $600,000, demonstrating sustained collector interest in original works.

### Community and Adoption

The Pepe NFT projects have cultivated exceptionally engaged communities centered around both artistic expression and internet culture:

**Rare Pepe Platform**: The Rare Pepe Wallet removed technical entry barriers to creating crypto art, onboarding hundreds of users and first-time collectors without requiring advanced technical knowledge. This democratization generated hundreds of trading cards, evolving the meme's iconography while creating new blockchain histories and personalities.

**Fake Rares Community Dynamics**: Fake Rares began organically within the Rare Pepe Telegram channel as artists were rejected by or not submitted to the original Rare Pepe curatorial committee (known as the "scientists" in community terminology). The initial Series 0 of Fake Rares consisted entirely of artworks originally intended for Rare Pepe but didn't make the cut, demonstrating how the community evolved rather than fractured.

**Key Artists and Figures**: Notable figures shaping both movements include Rare Scrilla (founder of Fake Rares), Joe Looney (wallet creator), and contemporary artists like Indelible, Robness, Pepenardo, Dim Zayan, and mera.ki. These creators bridge the communities across different blockchain platforms and eras.

**Community-Driven Value**: The ecosystem exemplifies how NFT value derives as much from community perception and shared cultural narratives as from scarcity itself. Inside jokes and community-driven narratives function as core value mechanisms.

### Strategic Partnerships

The Pepe ecosystem has attracted institutional attention and collaborative initiatives:

**Auction Houses**: Artnet Auctions has hosted dedicated on-chain sales celebrating Rare and Fake Pepe movements, legitimizing these projects within mainstream art market institutions.

**Museum Collections**: Sotheby's inclusion of Rare Pepes in their "Natively Digital" auction series signals recognition of these works as significant crypto art historical artifacts.

**Cross-Platform Bridging**: Vincent Van Dough's Notable Pepes project explicitly aims to "bridge the gap" between Pepe communities on both Counterparty and Ethereum, facilitating artist collaboration and collector access across blockchain ecosystems.

### Risks and Challenges

The ecosystem operates within regulatory uncertainty surrounding NFT classification and token utilities. Additionally, sustainability of community engagement and artist participation remains a perpetual consideration for projects reliant on cultural narratives and insider knowledge. Market volatility affecting both Bitcoin and crypto-native assets directly impacts collector participation and secondary market trading activity.

Competition from newer NFT projects and the challenge of maintaining cultural relevance in a rapidly evolving digital art landscape present ongoing obstacles. The reliance on Counterparty protocol, while philosophically aligned with Bitcoin maximalism, may limit accessibility compared to more mainstream Ethereum-based platforms.

### Future Outlook

The Pepe NFT ecosystem shows signs of sustained evolution and longevity beyond initial market cycles. Fake Rares' continuation into its fourth year demonstrates enduring community commitment beyond speculative phases. The emergence of derivative projects like Notable Pepes indicates that the Pepe narrative continues expanding rather than stagnating.

The return to Bitcoin's Counterparty protocol by Fake Rares reflects broader philosophical shifts in the NFT space regarding blockchain choice and values alignment. As newer systems like Bitcoin's Rune tokens emerge with utilities similar to historical Pepe Cash systems, the foundational innovations of Rare Pepe and Fake Rares appear increasingly prescient.

The cultural resilience of Pepe as a meme and the demonstrated artist commitment across multiple project iterations suggest the PEPEVERSE will likely continue evolving, with community creativity and authenticity remaining core differentiators in an increasingly commercialized NFT landscape.

## Relay Protocol

### Project Overview

Relay Protocol is a multichain payments network designed to facilitate fast, low-cost asset bridging and transactions across over 85 blockchains. It functions as a cross-chain interoperability platform that enables users and applications to seamlessly move assets and execute transactions across different blockchain ecosystems. Rather than requiring users to navigate each blockchain individually, Relay abstracts away the complexities of a multichain environment, allowing developers to create unified experiences where users can interact with multiple chains through a single interface.

Since launching in 2024, Relay has achieved significant scale. The platform now serves over 5 million users, has processed more than 50 million transactions, and has moved over $5-6 billion in total transaction volume. On Ethereum alone, Relay routes an average of 3 transactions every block, representing nearly 0.7% of all network activity.

### Three-Layered Infrastructure Stack

Relay's infrastructure is built on a comprehensive three-layered stack that serves different audiences. This core infrastructure powers all products and services built on top of it.

### Core Technological Pillars

The protocol employs two primary technological components:

1. **Cross-Chain Intent System**: Enables users to express their desired outcomes across chains without managing chain-specific complexities
2. **DEX Meta-Aggregation**: Executes transactions efficiently across decentralized exchanges to optimize pricing and routing

This dual-component approach is specifically designed to maintain low gas costs, support rapid expansion to new blockchain networks, and provide high reliability.

### Performance Optimizations

Relay achieves its speed and cost-efficiency through several key optimizations:

**Optimized Routing**: The platform uses sophisticated routing algorithms to select the most cost-effective and efficient pathways for bridging assets between chains.

**Pre-Funded Liquidity**: Rather than relying on waiting periods for cross-chain confirmations, Relay uses pre-funded liquidity pools to instantly fulfill swap and bridge requests.

**Gas Abstraction**: The platform automatically manages gas fees for users, topping up wallets with necessary tokens to cover transaction costs across different chains.

**Low-Cost Chain Leverage**: Relay strategically uses the lowest-cost chains for certain operations to minimize overall transaction costs.

### Technical Complexity

Supporting 85+ blockchains requires maintaining integrations with diverse technical architectures, consensus mechanisms, and upgrade cycles. Any breaking changes or vulnerabilities in supported chains could affect Relay's operations.

### Competition from Native Solutions

As blockchain ecosystems mature, native cross-chain solutions (such as Cosmos IBC, Polkadot parachains, or chain-specific bridges) may offer advantages in specific corridors, potentially fragmenting Relay's market position.
