### Perplexity Reason

Animoca Brands is a Hong Kong-based blockchain developer and investor that has emerged as a prominent player in the Web3 ecosystem, with significant recent developments reshaping its trajectory toward mainstream institutional adoption.

### Company Overview and Recent Developments

Animoca Brands was founded in 2011 as a mobile game developer but pivoted into the cryptocurrency and blockchain space in 2017[3]. The company has since established itself as a diversified digital assets conglomerate with investments across over 600 blockchain companies[1][3].

The most significant recent development is Animoca's plan to become a publicly-listed company. In November 2025, the company announced its intention to list on the Nasdaq through a reverse merger with Currenc Group, a Singapore-based fintech company[1][3]. The deal, which is expected to be finalized in late 2026 (subject to regulatory and shareholder approvals), will result in Animoca shareholders owning 95% of the merged entity while Currenc shareholders will own 5%[1]. According to co-founder Yat Siu, this will create "the world's first publicly-listed, diversified digital assets conglomerate"[1].

### Market Position

Animoca has carved out a distinctive niche within the crypto industry. Unlike recent crypto companies that went public through alternative listings (such as Circle and Gemini, which focus on exchanges and stablecoins), Animoca's business model centers on **altcoins**—cryptocurrencies other than Bitcoin and Ethereum[1]. Institutional-ready altcoins currently represent approximately one-quarter of the overall crypto market, positioning Animoca to serve as an institutional proxy for accessing this growing asset class[3].

The company's proposed Nasdaq listing will essentially function as an altcoin index vehicle, similar to MicroStrategy (MSTR) for Bitcoin or Sharplink (SBET) for other assets, but focused on a diversified basket of tokens[3]. This positioning addresses a critical institutional challenge: while major investors readily purchase Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana, accessing and selecting among hundreds of smaller altcoins remains difficult. Animoca offers a solution through its curated portfolio approach.

### Technology and Core Strategic Initiatives

#### Real-World Asset (RWA) Tokenization

Animoca's primary technological focus centers on tokenizing real-world assets, a sector experiencing rapid institutional adoption. The on-chain RWA market (excluding stablecoins) has grown to an estimated $24.8 billion, representing 57.9% year-to-date growth[6]. Animoca's research estimates a total addressable market of **$400 trillion** for RWA tokenization, encompassing commodities, equities, and alternative assets, with potential growth to $30 trillion by 2030[2].

RWA tokenization offers several structural advantages: it enables fractional ownership of traditionally illiquid assets, permits 24/7 trading on blockchain networks, and democratizes access to investments previously reserved for accredited investors[2]. These innovations represent a fundamental bridge between traditional finance (TradFi) and decentralized finance (DeFi).

#### Multichain Infrastructure

Rather than concentrating on a single blockchain, Animoca emphasizes **interoperability** across multiple chains as essential for long-term success[2]. While Ethereum currently dominates RWA tokenization with 55% market share and $156 billion in on-chain value, Animoca is actively developing infrastructure on high-performance alternatives like Solana and Aptos to mitigate network congestion risks and reduce regulatory concentration[2].

### Strategic Partnerships

Animoca has established several institutional-grade partnerships:

**ProvLabs Collaboration**: Animoca co-developed **NUVA**, a chain-agnostic vault marketplace built on the Provenance Blockchain, which currently holds $15.7 billion in RWA total value locked. NUVA launched with institutional products including nuYLDS (Figure Technologies' SEC-registered yielding stablecoin) and nuHELOCs (home equity loan pools)[2].

**Regulatory and Compliance Partnerships**: The company partnered with Standard Chartered and HKT to develop an HKD-backed stablecoin and established the joint venture **Anchorpoint** in Hong Kong, which is pursuing a stablecoin issuer license[2]. These partnerships demonstrate proactive regulatory alignment.

**Blockchain Ecosystem Partnerships**: Animoca's Moca Network partnerships include integrations with Plume and Soneium (Sony's blockchain arm), including an anime identity layer that taps into the $30 billion global anime market by tokenizing digital identities and creating new asset-backed yield opportunities[2].

### Portfolio Composition

Animoca's investment portfolio spans diverse sectors including Web3 gaming, decentralized identity systems, and real-world asset tokenization[3]. The company has notable past investments in blockchain gaming platforms, though some investments (such as Sandbox, a metaverse platform) have experienced significant challenges[1].

### Market and Operational Risks

Several risk factors warrant consideration:

**Investment Concentration and Volatility**: While Animoca's diversification across 600+ companies reduces single-project risk, the altcoin market exhibits substantially higher volatility than Bitcoin or Ethereum. Many altcoins have lower trading liquidity than Bitcoin, complicating large-scale acquisitions or liquidations[8].

**Portfolio Quality Risk**: Past investments demonstrate that even diversified portfolios cannot guarantee positive returns in the volatile Web3 space, as evidenced by underperforming metaverse platforms in Animoca's portfolio[1].

**Regulatory Uncertainty**: Although Animoca has pursued regulatory alignment through partnerships and compliance initiatives, the broader regulatory landscape for cryptocurrency remains uncertain. The proposed Nasdaq listing itself requires regulatory approval, introducing execution risk to the company's public market strategy[3].

**Market Timing Risk**: The IPO is targeted for late 2026, requiring 9-12 months for documentation finalization, board composition confirmation, merger ratio determination, shareholder approval, and regulatory clearance[3]. Cryptocurrency market conditions could shift substantially during this period.

**Technology and Competitive Risk**: The RWA tokenization and altcoin index space is attracting increasing competition from traditional financial institutions and other crypto platforms. Animoca's continued technological innovation and partnership development remain critical for maintaining market position[2].

Animoca Brands represents a significant institutional-focused play on altcoin and tokenized asset markets, with recent developments positioning it at the intersection of blockchain innovation and traditional finance participation.

The move addressed legitimate operational challenges. Prior to the suspension, Believe had received over 5,000 replies to Launchcoin's X post, though this paled in comparison to over 20,000 daily token launches on competing platforms like Pump.fun.[2]

Despite its innovation, Believe presents several significant risks that potential users and investors should carefully consider:

Believe occupies a unique niche within the broader 2025 cryptocurrency landscape, which has seen increasing institutional adoption and mainstream integration.[3] The platform positions itself at the intersection of meme culture and decentralized finance, targeting users interested in accessible token creation rather than sophisticated traders.

For potential investors, LAUNCHCOIN is characterized as a high-risk but potentially rewarding opportunity, particularly for those specifically interested in Web3 launchpad platforms and governance ecosystems.[4] The token's value proposition depends heavily on the platform's sustained success and ability to attract legitimate project launches. ### Perplexity Reason

Dolomite is a **next-generation decentralized money market and trading protocol** that combines the functionalities of lending protocols and decentralized exchanges into a single, integrated platform.[1][3] Created by Adam Knuckey and Corey Caplan in 2019 following their frustrations with existing cryptocurrency exchanges, the project received $2.5 million in initial funding from Draper Goren Holm and other investors to develop its infrastructure.[1]

### Recent Developments

The Dolomite project experienced significant momentum in 2025. Most notably, **Binance listed Dolomite (DOLO) spot trading on August 27, 2025**, at 16:00 UTC, with the token paired against USDT, USDC, BNB, FDUSD, and TRY, providing multiple trading pairs for users.[11] Shortly after, in late September 2025, Dolomite became the 33rd project featured in Binance's HODLer Airdrops program, distributing **15 million DOLO tokens (1.5% of max supply)** to BNB holders who met specific holding criteria between August 3-6, 2025.[5] The tokens were delivered directly to users' Spot Accounts prior to trading commencing.

According to projections available at the time, DOLO's price was anticipated to reach approximately **$0.06734 during 2025**, with analysts suggesting better opportunities for increases as market confidence grows.[13] The protocol has been characterized as undergoing a significant reinvention in 2025, transitioning from a simple margin and lending platform to establish itself as a comprehensive hub of liquidity.[9]

### Technology & Architecture

Dolomite's competitive advantage lies in its **modular two-layer architectural design** comprising a core immutable layer and a module mutable layer.[4] This design enables future upgrades and expansions without disrupting existing functionality. The protocol leverages a **virtual liquidity system** that enhances capital efficiency and potentially reduces borrowing rates while maintaining security standards.[1]

The platform integrates several key functionalities:

- **Over-Collateralized Loans**: Users can borrow by depositing collateral with higher value than the borrowed amount, providing protocol security.[1]
- **Margin Trading**: Borrowed funds can be used to amplify trading returns.[1]
- **Spot Trading**: Direct peer-to-peer cryptocurrency trading between users.[1]
- **Integrated Trading and Lending**: Users can deposit assets, borrow other assets, and trade all within single transactions, eliminating the need to bridge between platforms and reducing gas fees.[3]

One of Dolomite's defining characteristics is its **extensive token support**. Unlike conventional DeFi lending protocols that restrict collateral to major cryptocurrencies and stablecoins, Dolomite supports **over 1,000 distinct tokens**, including established digital assets, liquidity provider positions, and long-tail community coins.[2] This approach activates previously idle capital from emerging projects and smaller communities while maintaining security through rigorous risk management protocols that leverage Chainlink price oracles.[8]

### Market Position

Dolomite positions itself as the **"center of DeFi activities,"** enabling protocols, yield aggregators, DAOs, market makers, and hedge funds to easily manage assets and execute on-chain strategies.[4] The platform distinguishes itself through its capital-efficient model, which allows users to leverage collateral simultaneously for both borrowing and trading without moving assets between different platforms.

The protocol's approach to **user asset sovereignty** differentiates it from traditional finance arrangements. ### Technology & Architecture

**Core Architecture**

EOS is built on the Graphene blockchain technology, a foundational framework that provides fast transaction speeds, high throughput, strong stability, and complete functionality.[1] The EOS code framework consists of four interconnected layers: the library function layer (implementing core technologies like transaction processing and encryption), the plugin layer (allowing dynamic plugin combination), the contract layer (deploying smart contracts), and the application layer (providing external service interfaces).[1]

The EOS system comprises six major components: the P2P network communication component, state management and data block component, consensus algorithm component, account management component, transaction and contract component, and smart contract execution environment component.[1]

**Key System Components**

The EOS ecosystem operates through three primary applications:[1]

- **Nodeos**: The blockchain node component running on servers, responsible for launching the EOS node service, handling account management, block generation, consensus establishment, and providing a smart contract execution environment
- **Keosd**: The wallet management program managing wallets, keys, and transaction signing
- **Cleos**: A standard command-line client tool with powerful extensibility used to interact with both Nodeos and Keosd through the HTTP protocol

**Consensus Mechanism**

EOS utilizes a Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS) consensus mechanism with Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT), combining two separate layers within the EOSIO software stack.[3] Layer 1 implements asynchronous Byzantine Fault Tolerance (aBFT) for block production and validation, while Layer 2 handles Delegated Proof of Stake for producer voting and scheduling.[3] This approach allows validators to be scheduled to verify blocks in a specific order, differing from traditional Proof-of-Work systems like Bitcoin.

### Market Position

EOS has been regarded as a strong competitor to Ethereum and is classified as a representative of Blockchain 3.0.[1] The platform aims to become a more performant operating system supporting industrial-scale decentralized applications with the unique feature of zero transaction fees.[4] EOS offers advanced tools that allow developers to abstract blockchain elements away from end-users, creating a Web2-like experience with Web3 features.[7]

### Recent Developments

**Community Split and Governance**

The conflict escalated when Block.one sold 45 million tokens, and the top 25 EOS block producers voted to halt the distribution of 67 million vesting tokens (valued around $214 million) that were scheduled to be released to Block.one.[2]

On December 19, 2021, a deal was reached between the parties, with La Rose describing the separation as "clean."[2] He emphasized that the EOS community could now serve as its own product lead, independent of Block.one's control of the codebase and intellectual property.[2]

**Legal Action**

On February 17, 2022, the EOS Network Foundation announced plans to pursue legal action against Block.one, alleging that the organization "broke promises" to invest in the network and failed to follow through on commitments affecting network participants.[2] Given that the EOS blockchain protocol raised $4.1 billion from network participants, the eventual claim amount could be substantial.[2]

EOS is evolving into Vaulta, a development aimed at unlocking Bitcoin and digital assets while establishing a secure and scalable financial ecosystem for Web3 banking.[12]
